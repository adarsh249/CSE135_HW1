Analysis 1

Question:

Does the type of OS affect the page loading time for users under different types of network?

Intro:

The loading time of web pages is a crucial factor of user experience, it directly affects the satisfaction and management of website users. In addition, load times are incredibly important to increase accessibilty. There are many factors that could impact page loading time, one of the most important ones being the user network conditions users are accessing the internet from. We all know that 4G on average is much faster than 3G, but could a certain operating system be more efficient on a type of network than another? In this context, it is important to explore whether the type of OS affects the page loading time for users under different types of networks.

The above grid shows a table of raw data we obtained from user visiting our page. In this table we have their user sessionID, operating system, network type, window dimension and their accepted languages. This is a good representation of where we draw our data from. However, they are not presentable to users and it is hardly compelling in such form, and that is why we perform the below visualization for our analysis. However, it is important to note that this may help us learn more about a user since we would know their screen dimensions and possibly their geographic representation based off their languages. We also shredded the data down to include unique sessions only. These are all factors that may possibly affect load times.

The above pie chart depicts the percentage of users based on their operating system, we can tell that it is spread pretty evenly between MacOS and Windows and each operating system covers a substantial amount of users. This is why we need to investigate this trend as operating systems such as Windows, macOS, iOS, and Android have distinct architectures, functionalities, and optimizations that can potentially impact the performance of web page loading.

The above bar graph illustrates the variation in loading time solely based on the operating system. The information tells us that MacOS has the fastest loading time followed by android, and the slowest operating system was Windows. While this representation can provide valuable insights and be indicative of performance disparities, it is important to acknowledge the absence of network type. It is crucial to consider that not all users have access to high-speed internet connections or advanced routers. Therefore, the graph's findings may potentially be misleading, as the impact of network conditions on loading time is not accounted for, potentially underrepresenting real-world user experiences.

This graph is a more accurate representation of the real world situation. It is very obvious that 4G network users on average have a much faster loading time than that of the 3G users. However, there are some variations among their operating systems. For example, windows are slower on 3G networks while they are faster on 4G networks. Macs on the other hand are slower on 4G networks but faster on 3G networks. Android on the other hand had too little data and we failed to analyze anything meaningful from it. Unfortunately, we are not able to track IOS because of technical difficulties.

Conclusion:

The comprehensive evaluation of user performance is crucial for driving improvements in our website. It is essential to move beyond singular metrics such as operating system or network type and embrace a more holistic approach to obtain detailed and accurate results. Given additional time to enhance the analysis, two key enhancements would be considered. Firstly, incorporating data for iOS devices would provide a more complete representation of the user base. iOS has done a great job disabling any sort of tracking software, even when using Chrome on an iOS device. Having iOS data would better complete our metrics. Secondly, introducing an additional metric to track user device performance would further mitigate potential bias arising from variations in compute power. These augmentations would significantly contribute to a more robust and informative analysis, enabling us to make informed decisions and implement targeted optimizations for our website.
